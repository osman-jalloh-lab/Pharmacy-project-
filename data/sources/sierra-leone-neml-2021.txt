FOREWORD 
Ensuring medicines security for poor, vulnerable, and hard -to-reach 
communities is a priority of the Government of Sierra Leone. This 
remains a key driving force behind the Free Health Care Initiative 
currently in place principally for pregnant women, lactating mothers, and 
children under -five years old . However , meeting the pharmaceutical 
demands of the population poses a challenge to all countries especially 
low-income countries, which have several competing priorities. It is for these reasons that the use 
of the World Health Organization’s guidelines for the selection of Essential Medicines for public 
health sector provision becomes very critical for the management of the overall health sector 
budget. 
 
Whilst the public health needs and cost-effectiveness of these health products are very important 
factors that influenced the selection process, close attention was also paid to the safety and 
efficacy of the medicines. 
 
The approach of linking the revision of the Essential Medicines List to the revision of the 
treatment schedules for the National Basic Health Care Packages is an innovative one. We have 
also taken into consideration health data from the major health facilities in the country and the 
utilization of laboratory data on the susceptibility trends to the available antimicrobial agents. The 
approach will ensure a strong link between priority health needs and the Essential Medicines List. 
 
The year 2021 edition of the Essential Medicines List also contains the categorization of 
medicines according to their indications and healthcare levels of use. This is to promote rational 
medicines use and improved patient safety. 
 
It is hoped that the efforts of all stakeholders in ensuring medicines security for our population 
are achieved. 
  
Minister of Health and Sanitation 
Government of the Republic of Sierra Leone

PREFACE 
 
Essential medicines as defined by WHO are those medicines that satisfy the priority health needs 
of the population. The national Essential Medicines List (EML) is used to guide the importation 
and distribution, prescription, and dispensing of medicines in a country. The main users of the 
Essential Medicines List are the Medicines Control Authority for import control and managers of 
the public pha rmaceutical supply chain management system. The national EML also informs 
clinicians on medicines available in the public health sector medicines supply chain. 
 
For the national Essential Medicines List to fully serve the intended purposes, it should be revised 
every three years to accommodate new developments in the international market and the changing 
public health demands. However, medicines for the management of emerging and re -emerging 
diseases will be addressed promptly when the need arises and in exceptional cases, application to 
obtain non -listed items could be considered by the Ministry of Health And Sanitat ion, on the 
advice of the Directorate of Pharmaceutical Services 
 
Another purpose of revising the national EML is to remove or re-classify products based on safety 
information generated locally or from other credible sources like the WHO. This  latest edition, 
which is a revision of the year 2016 Essential Medicines List, categorizes/classifies medicines by 
health care level of use.
